# Advocacy Strategy: Safer Highways India

## Goal
To build public and political momentum for the "1000 Points" highway safety reform plan, culminating in pilot implementation on 5 high-risk corridors.

## Target Audience
1. **Policymakers:** MoRTH officials, NHAI leadership, MPs from constituencies with high accident rates.
2. **Influencers:** Road safety NGOs (SaveLIFE Foundation), urban planners, transport journalists.
3. **Public:** Commuters, truck drivers, families affected by road accidents.

## Phase 1: Awareness (Months 1-3)
- **Launch Website & Policy Brief:** Share the "1000 Points" plan widely.
- **Social Media Campaign:** Use #SaferHighwaysIndia to share infographics and statistics.
- **Op-Ed Series:** Pitch articles to major newspapers (The Hindu, Indian Express) summarizing the plan.
- **Webinar:** Host a panel discussion with road safety experts discussing the "1000 Points" concept.

## Phase 2: Engagement (Months 4-6)
- **Petition:** Launch a Change.org petition demanding pilot implementation.
- **MP Outreach:** Send personalized policy briefs to MPs, highlighting accident stats in their districts.
- **Partner with NGOs:** Collaborate with organizations like SaveLIFE Foundation to endorse the plan.

## Phase 3: Action (Months 7-12)
- **Pilot Proposal:** Formally submit a detailed pilot proposal to MoRTH for 5 specific corridors.
- **Public Accountability Boards:** Advocate for mandatory display of engineer names on new highway projects.
- **Legal Advocacy:** Work with lawyers to draft amendments for the 1-Hour Highway Court system.

## Key Messages
- **"Highways are for movement, not chaos."** (Focus on restricted access)
- **"Justice delayed is safety denied."** (Focus on 1-Hour Courts)
- **"Accountability saves lives."** (Focus on engineering liability)

## Metrics for Success
- Number of policy brief downloads.
- Social media engagement (shares, comments).
- Endorsements from key stakeholders.
- Meetings secured with MoRTH/NHAI officials.
